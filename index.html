Why your MyInvois e-invoice gets rejected

The MyInvois JSON format is strict UBL 2.1. The most common reasons a file is rejected:

  • Values not wrapped as arrays — it must be "ID": [{ "_": "INV-001" }], not "ID": "INV-001".
  • Currency written as RM instead of the ISO code MYR.
  • Missing supplier or buyer TIN, or a TIN that isn’t 14 characters.
  • Wrong date/time format — date must be YYYY-MM-DD, time HH:MM:SSZ in UTC.
  • A single transaction over RM10,000 sent as a consolidated / general-public invoice (not allowed since Jan 2026).
  • currencyID on an amount not matching the document currency.

FAQ

Is my invoice data uploaded anywhere?

No. Validation runs entirely in your browser using JavaScript. Your JSON never leaves your device — there is no server and nothing is stored. You can disconnect from the internet and it still works.

Does this submit my e-invoice to LHDN?

No. This only checks the structure and format of your JSON. You still submit through the official MyInvois portal or API. This tool helps you catch errors first so the submission doesn’t bounce.

What does it check?

The wrapped UBL field format, presence of the mandatory Invoice v1.1 fields (ID, dates, type code, currency, supplier/buyer TIN & registration ID, MSIC, address, line items, tax total, legal monetary total), and the common format mistakes the portal rejects. It does not verify digital signatures or do a live TIN lookup.

Is this the official MyInvois validator?

No. It is an independent, unofficial helper. The authoritative validation is always the official MyInvois system.
